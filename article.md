# 🔐 Incident Response in a Security Operations Center (SOC)

## Introduction
Today most organizations use computers, networks, cloud services, and web applications to run their business. Because of this, cyberattacks are also increasing.

To protect systems from these threats, organizations use a **Security Operations Center (SOC)**.

![SOC Workflow](images/soc_workflow.png)

*Figure 1: Security Operations Center workflow showing monitoring, detection, and response.*

---

## What is Incident Response?

Incident Response is a structured process used to handle cybersecurity incidents.

Examples of incidents include:

- Unauthorized access
- Malware infections
- Phishing attacks
- Data breaches
- Denial-of-Service (DoS)

---

## Incident Response Lifecycle

![Incident Response Lifecycle](images/incident_response_lifecycle.png)

*Figure 2: Incident Response lifecycle used by SOC teams.*

---

### 1. Preparation
Organizations create incident response plans and prepare security tools.

### 2. Detection and Analysis

SOC analysts monitor network logs and alerts.

![Cyber Attack Detection](images/cyber_attack_detection.png)

*Figure 3: Cyber attack detection process.*

---

### 3. Containment
The SOC team stops the attack from spreading.

### 4. Eradication
Malware or vulnerabilities are removed.

### 5. Recovery
Systems are restored to normal operation.

### 6. Post-Incident Review
Security teams analyze the incident to improve defenses.

---

## Real-Life Example

The **WannaCry ransomware attack (2017)** affected thousands of organizations worldwide. Companies with strong incident response plans quickly isolated infected systems and restored data from backups.

---

## Importance of Incident Response

1. Reduces cyberattack damage  
2. Protects sensitive data  
3. Improves cybersecurity defenses  
4. Maintains business operations  
5. Builds customer trust  

---

## Conclusion

Incident Response is a critical function of a **Security Operations Center**. By following structured processes and using monitoring tools, organizations can detect threats early and respond effectively.
